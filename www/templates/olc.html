Chronicle :
Great place for trekking in the midst of nature, deep inside the jungle, A guide along would be really helpful.Situated around 3 km on the road to Avalanche Cairn hill. This is an excellent spot for a picnic, must watch in day time only.
The entrance road to the bill is flanked by dense cypress trees.
This reserve gets its name because of a Cairn, piled stone circle – a pre-historical burial site of the tribal, and has the oldest Cyprus plantation. This reserve is one of the few surviving original walks that the Britishers enjoyed. During rains, you can easily slip on the ground covered with dried leaves. Wear good walking/trekking shoes. Obey all rules of a forest reserve. If you are observant, you can sight a variety of birds (depending on the time of the day and season) such as Nilgiri Laughing Thrush, Scimitar Babbler, Flycatchers, Fantails, Jungle Fowls, Sunbirds, Warblers, and so on.No cafeteria available.
