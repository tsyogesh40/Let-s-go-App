Chronicle:
It is commonly believed that the temple got its name ‘Pachaimalai’ meaning ‘Green Hill’ owing to the trees and greeneries, which is wrong. The actual reason is the presence of a spring(நீரூற்று) directly beneath the presiding Deity at the sanctum santorum. It can be understood from the fact that water being used in the temple is obtained from a bore well on top of the hill. During the drought years of 2001 – 2005, when the wells went dry in the areas around the hill, the water was available on top of the hill, owing to the presence of the spring. Duration : 8 min
